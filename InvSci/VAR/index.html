Accuracy, Reliability and Validity Lab

Generate experimental datasets by adjusting sample size, equipment precision, random error, systematic error and controlled variables. Analyse when results are accurate, reliable or valid.

Learning Intention

Students will understand that accuracy, reliability and validity describe different qualities of scientific data and experimental design.

Success Criteria

Students can interpret datasets and graphs to decide whether results are accurate, reliable and valid, then justify their judgement using evidence.

Run repeated trials, then compare the results with the known true value. Change the settings to test different data quality problems.

4. Interpretation feedback

Run trials to classify the dataset.

    Accuracy

    Accuracy describes how close a result is to the true or accepted value. A small percentage error usually means the result is more accurate.

    Reliability

    Reliability describes how consistent the repeated trials are. A small spread, range or standard deviation usually means the results are more reliable.

    Validity

    Validity describes whether the method actually tests the scientific question. Poorly controlled variables can make an investigation invalid even if the numbers look neat.

    Student challenge

    Use the quick presets to create three different datasets:

    • accurate but not reliable
    • reliable but not accurate
    • invalid because of poor controlled variables

    For each dataset, use the graph, mean, standard deviation and percentage error to justify your judgement.

    Teacher notes

    • Accuracy focus: look at the distance between the mean and the true value.
    • Reliability focus: look at the spread of repeated trials, not whether the results are correct.
    • Validity focus: look at experimental design. A dataset can appear reliable but still be invalid if variables were not controlled.
    • Extension: students can export the CSV and calculate mean, range, standard deviation and percentage error in a spreadsheet.
