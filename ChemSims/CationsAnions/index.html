Classroom rule

Metals usually lose electrons to form positive cations. Non-metals often gain electrons to form negative anions. Noble gases already have full outer shells and are usually unreactive.

Model limits

This model uses shell diagrams to make ion formation easier to visualise. Real electrons do not orbit like tiny planets. Some elements in the first 20, especially hydrogen, carbon, silicon and the noble gases, need extra care because their real chemical behaviour is more complex than a simple gain-or-lose electron model.
