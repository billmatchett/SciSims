Peppered Moth Evolution Simulator

Model the classic peppered moth example of natural selection. The population begins in a clean, light-barked environment, then pollution darkens the tree bark, and later the environment becomes cleaner again. Survival advantage shifts as camouflage changes.

Controls

The moths do not change colour during their own lifetime. Evolution is shown by changes in the percentage of light and dark moths across generations.

What the model represents

Variation

The population contains both light and dark moths. This inherited variation is already present before selection occurs.

Environmental change

When pollution darkens bark, the trait with the survival advantage changes. When bark lightens again, the advantage can shift back.

Natural selection

Predators are more likely to catch moths that stand out. Better-camouflaged moths are more likely to survive and reproduce.

Inheritance and mutation

Offspring usually match their parent colour. Rare mutation can switch colour and add variation to the next generation.
