Classic Pong Focus Challenge

Control the paddle, return the ball and try to outscore the computer. Use repeated trials to investigate focus, reaction time, practice and how difficulty changes performance.

Learning Intention

Students will understand that performance can be investigated by collecting repeated trial data under controlled conditions.

Success Criteria

Students can collect scores, compare trials, calculate simple averages and explain how difficulty or practice affects performance.

Pong game

Choose a difficulty, press Start game, then move your paddle with the arrow keys.
Controls: Use Arrow Up and Arrow Down to move your paddle. First player to 7 wins the round.

Student challenge

  • Complete five trials on the same difficulty setting.
  • Record the player score, computer score and best rally.
  • Calculate your mean player score.
  • Repeat on a harder difficulty setting and compare the results.
  • Identify the independent variable, dependent variable and controlled variables.
  • Explain whether practice appears to improve performance.

Teacher notes

This activity can be used as a quick Working Scientifically or brain break investigation. Students can collect repeated trial data and compare performance across difficulty settings or practice attempts.

Suggested investigation question: How does difficulty affect player score in Pong? The independent variable is the difficulty setting. The dependent variables could include player score, result or best rally. Controlled variables include game length, winning score, player, device, screen size, control method and number of trials.
