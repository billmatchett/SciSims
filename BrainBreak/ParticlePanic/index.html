Particle Panic: Kinetic Energy Challenge

Control the glowing particle, collect tokens and avoid high-energy particles. Change the temperature to see how particle speed affects movement, collisions and survival time.

Learning Intention

Students will understand that increasing temperature increases the average kinetic energy and movement speed of particles.

Success Criteria

Students can collect repeated trial data, compare survival time and collisions, and explain how temperature affects particle motion.

Particle chamber

Choose a temperature, press Start game, then use the arrow keys to move your glowing particle.
Controls: Arrow keys to move. Collect yellow tokens. Avoid red high-energy particles.
Model note: This is a game model. Real particles move randomly and are not controlled by a player. Cold particles move more slowly. Hot particles move faster because they have greater average kinetic energy.

Student challenge

  • Complete three trials at room temperature.
  • Complete three trials at hot or extreme heat.
  • Compare tokens collected, collisions and survival time.
  • Identify the independent variable, dependent variables and controlled variables.
  • Explain how temperature affected particle movement.
  • Evaluate whether repeated trials made the results more reliable.

Teacher notes

This game links a brain break activity to the particle model of matter. Higher temperature settings increase the speed of background particles, making collisions more likely and the game harder.

Suggested investigation question: How does temperature affect survival time and collisions in a particle model game? The independent variable is the temperature setting. Dependent variables include tokens collected, number of collisions and survival time. Controlled variables include round length, player, device, screen size, control method and number of trials.

This is a simplified model. Real particles do not have goals, points or player control, but the movement pattern helps students visualise the idea that hotter particles move faster on average.
