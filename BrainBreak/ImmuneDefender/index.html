Immune Defender: Pathogen Chase

Control a white blood cell, remove pathogens and avoid harmful toxin zones. Compare how immune protection mode affects pathogen removal, toxin hits, cell health and survival time.

Learning Intention

Students will understand that the immune system protects the body by detecting and responding to pathogens.

Success Criteria

Students can use a model to collect data, compare immune response conditions and explain limitations of the model.

Immune system game

Choose an immune protection mode, press Start game, then use the arrow keys to move your white blood cell.
Controls: Arrow keys to move. Touch green pathogens to remove them. Avoid purple toxin zones.
Model note: This is a simplified game model. Prior immunity or vaccination is represented as faster recognition and response, shown by faster movement and a larger removal radius. Real immune responses involve many cells, antibodies and chemical signals.

Student challenge

  • Complete three trials with no prior immunity.
  • Complete three trials with strong vaccine protection.
  • Compare pathogens removed, toxin hits, cell health and survival time.
  • Identify the independent variable, dependent variables and controlled variables.
  • Explain how protection mode affected the immune response in the model.
  • Evaluate one strength and one limitation of this model.

Teacher notes

This game links a brain break activity to immune system modelling. The player represents a white blood cell. Green particles represent pathogens. Purple particles represent harmful toxin zones or tissue damage conditions that reduce cell health.

Suggested investigation question: How does immune protection mode affect pathogen removal, toxin hits and cell health? The independent variable is the protection mode. Dependent variables include pathogens removed, toxin hits, health remaining and survival time. Controlled variables include round length, player, device, screen size, control method and number of trials.

In this model, prior immunity and vaccination are represented by faster response speed and a larger pathogen removal radius. This is intended to represent quicker immune recognition and response, not a physical shield. Real immune responses involve barriers, white blood cell types, antibodies, antigens, memory cells, inflammation and chemical signalling.
