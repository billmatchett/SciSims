Colour-Word Interference Challenge

Name the colour of the word, not the word itself. Collect repeated trial data to investigate focus, reaction time, errors, interference and the effect of practice.

Learning Intention

Students will understand that attention and response speed can be investigated using repeated trials and controlled conditions.

Success Criteria

Students can collect accuracy and response-time data, calculate simple averages and explain how interference may affect performance.

Stroop-style challenge

Click the button that matches the ink colour of the word. Ignore what the word says.

Student challenge

  • Complete three trials using the matching-only mode.
  • Complete three trials using the interference-only mode.
  • Compare accuracy and mean response time for both modes.
  • Identify the independent variable, dependent variable and controlled variables.
  • Explain whether the word meaning interfered with your response.
  • Evaluate whether repeated trials made the results more reliable.

Teacher notes

This game is based on the colour-word interference effect, commonly associated with the Stroop task. Students must respond to the ink colour rather than the written word. When these conflict, many people become slower or make more errors.

Suggested investigation question: How does colour-word interference affect response time and accuracy? The independent variable is the mode or level of interference. Dependent variables include accuracy, number of errors and mean response time. Controlled variables include number of questions, device, screen size, player, button layout, lighting, distractions and practice time.
