Snake Decision-Time Challenge

Guide the snake to collect food while avoiding walls and your own trail. Use the results to investigate decision-making, attention, practice, reaction time and repeated trial data.

Learning Intention

Students will understand that performance can be investigated by collecting repeated trial data under controlled conditions.

Success Criteria

Students can collect scores, compare survival times, calculate simple averages and explain how changing a variable affects performance.

Student challenge

  • Complete five trials using the same speed setting.
  • Record your score and survival time after each trial.
  • Calculate your mean score and mean survival time.
  • Repeat using a different speed setting and compare the results.
  • Identify the independent variable, dependent variable and at least two controlled variables.
  • Explain whether practice appears to improve performance.

Teacher notes

This activity can be used as a short Working Scientifically investigation into performance, reaction time, attention and decision-making. Students can compare repeated trials at the same speed, then change one variable such as speed setting or distraction level.

Suggested investigation question: How does snake speed affect final score and survival time? The independent variable is the speed setting. The dependent variables are score and survival time. Controlled variables include player, device, screen size, starting conditions, control method and number of trials.

The activity can also support discussion of reliability. A single score may not represent a player's usual performance, so repeated trials and mean values are more useful than one result.
