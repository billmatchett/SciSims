Convex lensA converging lens can form a real inverted image when the object is outside the focal length.
Concave lensA diverging lens is thinner in the centre and forms a virtual, upright and reduced image for a real object.
Focal pointParallel rays are focused towards, or appear to come from, the focal point.
Bifocal lensDifferent parts of the lens have different focal lengths, helping with different viewing distances.

Teacher note: this is a thin-lens classroom model. It is designed to support ray diagram reasoning rather than replace formal optical bench measurements.
