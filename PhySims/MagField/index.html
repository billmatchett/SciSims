Magnetic Field Visualiser

Visualise the magnetic field around a bar magnet using compass needles, field lines and a movable field probe.

Physics Magnetism Fields Compass directions

Learning Intention

Students will understand that a magnetic field exists around a magnet and that the field has both direction and relative strength.

Success Criteria

Students can identify field direction around a bar magnet, describe where the field is strongest, and use a probe to compare field strength at different positions.

Model note

This sim uses a simplified two-pole model to show field direction and relative strength. It is useful for pattern recognition, but it is not a precise map of a real bar magnet.

Blue needles show field direction. Curves show the field pattern outside the magnet. Orange probe measures the local field.

Student challenge

  • Move the probe close to each pole and compare the field strength.
  • Move the probe far away from the magnet and describe what happens to the field strength.
  • Rotate the magnet and explain how the compass needles respond.
  • Reverse the polarity and identify what changes in the field pattern.
  • Use the field rule to explain why the compass needles point away from the north pole and towards the south pole.

Teacher notes

The sim is designed to show the shape, direction and relative strength of the magnetic field around a bar magnet. It separates the idea of a field from the idea of contact force.

  • Good prompt: Where is the field strongest and how can you tell?
  • Good prompt: Why do the compass needles curve around rather than point straight across the whole screen?
  • Extension: Ask students to sketch the field before turning on the field-line layer.
