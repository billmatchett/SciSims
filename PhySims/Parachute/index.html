Parachute Drop Parameter Simulator

Change the mass, drop height, parachute size and air resistance to investigate how a parachute affects falling motion. The model uses gravity pulling down and drag force pushing up against the motion. Save trials, download the data and graph the results in a spreadsheet.

Model idea:
Weight pulls the payload down. Air resistance pushes up and increases as speed increases.

Drag force: Fd = 0.5 × air density × drag coefficient × area × speed²

Teaching note: Doubling the parachute diameter makes the area four times larger because area depends on radius squared. At zero air density, the parachute produces no drag.
