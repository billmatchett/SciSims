Magnetic Force Visualiser

Explore attraction, repulsion and how magnetic force changes as the gap between magnets changes.

Physics Forces Fields Distance graphs

Learning Intention

Students will understand that magnetic forces can act at a distance and that the size and direction of the force depend on gap distance, magnet strength and pole arrangement.

Success Criteria

Students can describe attraction and repulsion, compare magnetic force at different gaps, and use a force-distance graph to explain why magnetic force weakens rapidly.

Model note

This sim shows a classroom model of relative force, not exact newtons. The field display is an approximation and the visual intensity is scaled so students can still see changes when the mathematical value becomes very small.

Student challenge

  • Find a gap distance where the relative force is less than 10%.
  • Double the gap distance and record what happens to the force.
  • Change the pole arrangement and explain what changes and what stays the same.
  • Compare the 1/d^2, 1/d^3 and 1/d^4 models. Which one drops off the fastest?
  • Use the compass map to describe how the magnetic field direction changes around the magnets.

Teacher notes

The distance slider controls the visible gap between facing poles. The compass needles show approximate magnetic field direction, while the large red arrows above the magnets show the net attraction or repulsion. The field layer is a teaching model rather than a precise map of a real bar magnet.

  • Good prompt: Why is the graph curved rather than straight?
  • Good prompt: Does reversing the poles change the size of the force or the direction of the force?
  • Extension: Ask students to sketch a predicted graph before running the sim.
