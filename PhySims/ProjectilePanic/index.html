Projectile Panic

Adjust launch angle, velocity, gravity and target distance to test projectile motion. Calculate first, launch second, then reveal the result after the projectile lands.

Learning Intention Students will understand that projectile motion can be modelled by separating horizontal and vertical motion.
Success Criteria Students can adjust projectile variables, interpret trajectory data and use evidence from the simulation to explain changes in range, height and time of flight.

Student challenge

  • Calculate launch settings that should hit the target within 2 m.
  • Change gravity to Mars and explain why the same launch settings behave differently.
  • Keep velocity constant and test three different angles. Identify which angle gives the greatest range.
  • Use the data table to explain the relationship between height, flight time and range.
  • Evaluate one limitation of this simulation compared with a real projectile experiment.

Teacher notes

This version uses an ideal projectile model with no air resistance or wind. Horizontal velocity is constant, while vertical velocity changes due to gravity.

Useful prompts: What variables affect horizontal range? Why does changing gravity affect both height and time of flight? Why might a real projectile not match this model exactly?
