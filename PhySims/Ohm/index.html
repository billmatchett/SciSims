Graph interpretation

Record at least five readings, then press Analyse graph.
Classify the current component after you collect enough data.
Independent variableThe voltage is changed deliberately using the power supply.
Dependent variableThe current is measured to see how the component responds.
Ohmic componentAn ohmic component has a straight-line I-V graph through the origin because resistance is constant.
Non-ohmic componentA non-ohmic component has a curved or threshold-shaped I-V graph because resistance changes with conditions such as temperature or direction.

Teacher note: students can collect negative and positive voltage readings to compare symmetrical ohmic behaviour with the curved response of a filament lamp, the one-way behaviour of a diode, and more difficult unknown components.
