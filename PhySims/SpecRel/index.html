Special Relativity Light Clock Simulator

Explore how time dilation appears when a spaceship moves close to the speed of light. Compare the traveller's light clock with the same clock as observed from a stationary frame.

Learning Intention

Students will understand that time measurements can differ between observers moving relative to one another at very high speeds.

Success Criteria

Students can explain how a light clock model shows time dilation and use the Lorentz factor to compare time measured in different frames of reference.

  2. Set the spaceship speed to 90% of the speed of light. Record the Lorentz factor and traveller time.
  3. Set the spaceship speed to 99% of the speed of light. What happens to the traveller's time compared with the stationary observer's time?
  4. Use the graph to explain why time dilation is not noticeable for cars, planes or normal rockets.

Teacher Notes

This sim is a conceptual model of special relativity. It focuses on time dilation using a light clock rather than a full mathematical derivation.

  • Key idea: The speed of light is treated as constant for all observers.
  • Traveller frame: The light moves straight up and down between the mirrors.
  • Stationary observer frame: The clock moves sideways while the light travels, so the observed light path is diagonal.
  • Formula used: gamma = 1 / sqrt(1 - v²/c²), and traveller time = stationary observer time / gamma.
  • Discussion point: Relativistic effects become significant only when speeds are a large fraction of the speed of light.
