Lift the Load

Use six simple machines to lift the same heavy crate. Adjust the design, compare effort force and distance, then decide whether the machine really made the task easier.

Learning Intention

Students will understand that simple machines can reduce or redirect the effort force needed to lift a load, but this usually increases the distance over which the effort force must be applied.

Success Criteria

Students can use force, distance, work, mechanical advantage and efficiency data to compare simple machines and explain the trade-off between effort force and effort distance.

Student Challenge

  1. Choose one simple machine and lift the crate to the platform.
  2. Keep the effort force below the target value.
  3. Record at least one successful trial for three different machines.
  4. Identify the best balance of low effort force and reasonable effort distance.
  5. Explain why the machine did not remove the need to do work.

Assess the Simulation

  • What did this simulation help you understand?
  • What is one strength of the simulation as a model?
  • What is one limitation compared with a real machine?
  • How could friction, flexing, slipping, or measurement error affect a real investigation?
  • Overall, how useful was this simulation for explaining mechanical advantage?

Teacher Notes

Use the sim to emphasise that ideal simple machines trade effort force for effort distance. The model uses simplified mechanical advantage values and then applies a friction loss.

Core relationship: work = force x distance.
